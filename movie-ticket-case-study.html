01 · Project Overview

Booking for a group, without the group headache.

CINE reimagines the movie ticket booking experience for families, couples, and friend groups. Built as a mobile-first iOS app, it challenges incumbents like BookMyShow and District by making group seat selection effortless, transparent, and fast.

A native iOS movie ticket booking app focused on group and family outings.

Reduce booking time and decision fatigue for group seat selection.

Families, couples, and friend groups aged 25–40 booking weekend entertainment.

Compete with established apps by solving a specific pain point: finding adjacent seats.

02 · Assumptions

What we believed before we designed.

Business Assumptions

  • Users are looking to buy via a flexible, reliable app.
  • Bookings should be seamless and discoverable within the app.
  • Checkout should take less than 2 minutes from seat selection to payment.
  • Group bookings represent a high-value, repeat-use segment.

User Assumptions

  • Typical booking unit is a family or couple, often 2 adults + 2 children.
  • Users plan weekend outings in advance, usually 2–7 days ahead.
  • Most users do not know which seats offer the best view or audio experience.
  • Users want minimal steps, transparent pricing, and fast checkout.
  • Families and groups strongly prefer sitting together.
03 · Competitor Analysis

BookMyShow and District set the stage.

Two dominant players shape user expectations in India. CINE finds its edge in the gaps they leave open.

BookMyShow

Market leader with the widest cinema coverage and deepest showtime data. Group booking still forces users to hunt for adjacent seats manually, and convenience fees are often revealed late.

District

Experience-first challenger with strong branding and discovery features. Smaller reach and fewer transparent pricing cues leave room for a faster, family-focused booking flow.

CINE Opportunity

Smart seat selection for groups, transparent pricing from the first screen, and a checkout path built specifically around the family-outing use case.

05 · Pain Points

What current apps get wrong.

01

Adjacent seats are hard to find

Users hunting multiple tickets often struggle to find nearby seats, especially for popular shows or prime timings.

02

Hidden convenience fees

Additional charges such as convenience fees are added late in the flow, creating a perception of hidden costs and frustration.

03

Too many decisions

From comparing showtimes to manually choosing seats, the number of micro-decisions makes the booking process feel heavy.

07 · Innovative Feature

Smart Seat Selection.

The core differentiator is a Smart Seat Selection System that automatically recommends the best available adjacent seats based on group size, seat preferences, and real-time availability.

  • Users select how many tickets they need.
  • The system finds the best adjacent seats with optimal viewing angle and audio experience.
  • Recommendations are surfaced clearly with a single-tap accept action.
  • Users can still choose manually if they prefer a different section.
  • Pricing and convenience fees are transparent from the start.
