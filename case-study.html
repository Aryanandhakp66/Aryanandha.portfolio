01 - Overview

A national app, one missing detail.

DigiYatra is the paperless airport experience used by millions across India. The technology works. The home screen, however, buries the single most important detail of any travel day - the flight time - forcing travellers back to email, WhatsApp, or a paper printout to confirm what the app was meant to surface.

This redesign rebuilds the boarding-pass surface around one principle: critical information should be readable in under one second - the time it takes to glance at a wristwatch.

02 - The spark

A real complaint, from a real traveller.

A LinkedIn post from a frequent flyer - published months after the app's national rollout - captured the exact friction the product was meant to remove. It was a single sentence, but it named the problem precisely.

03 - What users struggle with

Three frictions, one flat hierarchy.

Each of these breaks down the trust users have in the app at the most stressful moment of their day - minutes before boarding.

  • 01 - Visibility

    Flight timing not visible

    The most important data point - boarding and departure time - is buried or missing entirely from the home view.

  • 02 - Context-switching

    Forced to leave the app

    Users open WhatsApp, Gmail, or a paper copy to find the time they expected DigiYatra to surface.

  • 03 - Hierarchy

    Everything looks equally important

    Seat, gate, PNR and flight number are listed without any hierarchy - every element competes for the same attention.

04 - Design goals

Two goals. Each design decision had to defend itself.

Before opening Figma, a small, opinionated set of goals. No ambiguity, no scope creep - every pixel had to answer to this list.

Goal 01

Improve glanceability.

Critical info readable in under one second - the time it takes to glance at a wristwatch.

Goal 02

Surface critical info instantly.

Time, route, and gate above the fold. No taps, no scrolls, no guesswork.

05 - Before / After

Same data. Different decision.

The redesign doesn't add or remove information - it re-ranks it. Time leads. Everything else falls in line behind it.

Final Design
