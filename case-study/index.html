01 · Project Overview

An editorial storefront, in your pocket.

Zyra is a mobile-first shopping experience for handbags, accessories and lifestyle pieces. It blends the curation of a concept boutique with the speed of modern e-commerce - a place where discovery feels intentional and checkout feels effortless.

A native iOS shopping app for a premium fashion & lifestyle brand.

Convert browsing into ownership without losing the editorial atmosphere.

Style-conscious women, 22–38, who shop emotionally and value craft.

Translate a print-magazine sensibility into a touch-first interface.

02 · The Problem

Most fashion apps sell products.
Few sell the feeling.

Through interviews and reviews across leading fashion apps, three recurring frustrations surfaced - and quietly defined the brief.

01

The wishlist grows past browsing

Saved pieces pile up across weeks - by date, price, and stock state. Without a way to re-arrange them, finding the right bag at the right moment becomes work.

02

The wishlist is a graveyard

Items are saved at the "maybe later" moment - then forgotten. Without a nudge, the path back to those bags goes cold.

03

Checkout breaks the spell

Dense address forms and card fields rip users out of a carefully built product flow - right at the moment of purchase.

03 · Design Goals

Five intentions that shaped every pixel.

01

Simplify the shopping flow

Reduce taps between discovery and ownership. Let users move at the speed of impulse.

02

Craft a premium aesthetic

Editorial typography, generous whitespace, and a palette borrowed from a tailored leather atelier.

03

Improve product discovery

Pair editorial curation with intelligent categories so the right piece finds the right person.

04

Elevate the wishlist into a wardrobe

A second home for saved pieces - organised, revisitable, ready to convert.

05

Build an emotional connection

Treat every screen as a page in a lookbook. The brand is the product as much as the bag is.

09 · UX Decisions

Choices, explained.

Three moments where the design did something specific - and why.

- Decision 01 / Wishlist Filter

Re-ordering the wishlist by intent

A growing wishlist needs a way to surface the right piece at the right moment. A focused filter sheet lets users re-arrange the saved set by how they're thinking - newest first, by price, or by what's back in stock - without losing the wishlist context behind it.

  • Sort by Date Saved - revisit pieces in the order they caught the eye
  • Price · Low to High and Price · High to Low for budget-led decisions
  • Restock Status surfaces previously sold-out bags that are available again
- Decision 02 / Wishlist Reminders

Bringing users back to the bags they love

Shoppers save pieces to the wishlist at the "maybe later" moment - and many never come back. A personalised reminder layer gently re-surfaces saved bags, restocks, price drops and new exclusive collections - elegant, helpful, on-brand. Never intrusive.

  • Emotional recall copy: "Don't forget your wishlist - the bags you love are still waiting for you."
  • Grouped into Wishlist, New Arrivals, Orders, Updates - no notification fatigue
  • Large product imagery and clear CTAs land the purpose at a glance
- Decision 03 / Checkout

Three steps, one rhythm

Checkout is split across three calm screens - address, payment, review - instead of one dense form. The progress indicator borrows from a couture fitting: deliberate, paced, never rushed. Drop-off in usability tests fell sharply on a three-step layout.

  • Inline validation; errors never surface as red toasts
  • Saved methods recognised on return without re-entry
  • Order summary persists on every step - total never hides
